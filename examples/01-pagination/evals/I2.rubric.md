# UX rubric — I2 (judge eval, gate=flag)

Judge the paging experience against this rubric. The model is NOT called in this sandbox
(no `ANTHROPIC_API_KEY`); `../judge_sample_verdict.json` shows the verdict CONTRACT this eval
produces (structured rubric in → `{verdict, rationale}` out), so the judge tier is demonstrated by
its interface, not by a faked model call.

Rubric (PASS = all three):
1. No visible duplicate items while paging/scrolling.
2. No skipped or "jumping" items when new content arrives at the top.
3. Next-page loads are smooth and preserve order.

`gate=flag`: a FAIL surfaces as an advisory quality signal but does NOT block the slice, and this
eval is **excluded from the trust requirement** — an LLM-judge rubric does not deterministically
guard intent the way the `session.py` contract eval does (§5.6 of FRAMEWORK.md). For the gate that
blocks, see the trusted journey eval.
